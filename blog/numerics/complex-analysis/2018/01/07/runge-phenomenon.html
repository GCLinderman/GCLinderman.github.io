Error estimates

For a fixed $x$ and $f$, we want to know how the error estimate in the Hermite integral formula will change with the number of interpolation points $n$. In that integrand, $\frac{f(t)}{(x-t)}$ will not change with $n$. On the other hand,

will change dramatically based on $n$. We will have exponential convergence to $p(x)$ as long as the contour $\Gamma$ is farther from the interpolation points than $x$. We can extend $\Gamma$ only as far as it can be analytically continued. That is, convergence of polynomial interpolants to analytic functions on $[-1,1]$ is determined by how big $\ell(x)$ is on $\Gamma$ on which which is $f$ is analytic, compared to how small it is on $[-1,1]$.

You are only going to evaluate the $\ell(x)$ in the numerator at $x \in [-1,1]$. You want the denominator to be big, which will happen by letting $\Gamma$ be a bigger curve. How big can $\Gamma$ be? It is restricted to the region where $f$ is analytic.
